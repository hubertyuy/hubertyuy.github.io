Using Android Beam

By lightly touching together two devices that support Near Field Communication (NFC), images or web pages displayed on one device’s screen can be shown on the other device, and email information, calendar, pictures, contact information, XML information, etc, can be rapidly transferred.



Activating Android Beam

1Select “Settings” in the app menu or on the high speed settings page.

2Select “More”.

3Select “NFC”, and make sure the box is checked.

The NFC function is now activated, and “Android Beam” is a selectable setting.

4Select “Android Beam” and toggle the “on/off” switch in the upper right corner of the screen to the “on” position.

Android Beam will now activate.

Using Android Beam

1On an Android OS device that supports the NFC function, switch the Android Beam setting to “on”.

2Display the webpage, image, or other content that you would like to transfer on your device or another device that supports NFC.

3Place the NFC antenna of this device close to the NFC antenna of the other device.

This device’s NFC antenna is located in the device’s lower portion.



4 Select the transmitting and receiving screen.

The content on the transmitting device’s screen will be sent to the receiving device, and displayed on that device’s screen.

If the app is not installed on the receiving device, the receiving display will be prompted to download the app from Google Play.

The notification board will display content transfer and completion status.

　

Note

  • Do not use excessive force when touching this device to another NFC compatible device.
  • Slowly place this device near the device that will be receiving or transferring through NFC.
  • If transferring remains unsuccessful after touching this device to the target receiving device, try moving the devices closer, farther, or to the left or right.
  • When metal objects are placed between two transferring devices, this may influence NFC information transfer. Additionally, protective phone casings may influence NFC information transfer.
